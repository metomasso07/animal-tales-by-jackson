Al the Alligator

Story and art by Jackson, age 8

Meet Al, an alligator who lived in a peaceful swamp. Al had big teeth and a long tail, which made him look very scary. But Al wasn't scary at all‚Äîhe just wanted to make friends!

One warm day, Al spotted a family of ducks swimming near his favorite log. "Hello!" Al called out with a big smile.

But when the ducks saw Al's teeth, they quacked in fear and swam away as fast as they could!

Al felt sad. "Why won't anyone be my friend?" he wondered.

Later that day, Al heard a tiny voice. "Help! Help!" A baby turtle was stuck upside down on the riverbank, unable to flip over.

Al gently used his snout to flip the turtle right side up. "There you go, little friend!"

The baby turtle looked up at Al with big eyes. "Thank you! You're so kind! Would you like to be my friend?"

Al's heart filled with joy. "Really? You're not scared of me?"

"Of course not," said the turtle. "You just helped me! You're the nicest alligator I've ever met."

From that day on, Al and the turtle became best friends. Al learned that being kind is what really matters, not how you look!

Fun Fact: Alligators can live up to 50 years in the wild! They're excellent swimmers and can hold their breath underwater for up to an hour.
